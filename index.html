FAQ

Can I major in English if I'm not sure what career I want to pursue?

Absolutely! A degree in English offers valuable skills in critical thinking, communication, and analysis that are applicable to a wide range of career paths. Many of our graduates go on to pursue careers in fields such as publishing, education, law, marketing, and journalism. Our academic advisors can work with you to explore your interests and career goals and tailor your course of study to suit your aspirations.

How can I get involved in extracurricular activities related to English studies?

We offer a variety of extracurricular activities and organizations for students interested in English studies. From literary magazines and writing clubs to theater productions and academic conferences, there are plenty of opportunities to engage with your peers and faculty outside the classroom. Keep an eye on our event calendar and announcements for upcoming opportunities to get involved.

What resources are available to help me improve my writing skills?

Our Writing Center is dedicated to helping students improve their writing skills and succeed academically. Staffed by experienced tutors, the Writing Center offers one-on-one consultations, workshops, and resources to assist with all aspects of the writing process, from brainstorming and drafting to revising and editing. Whether you're working on a class assignment, research paper, or personal project, the Writing Center is here to support you.

Are there opportunities for undergraduate students to participate in research?

Yes! We encourage undergraduate students to engage in research and creative projects under the guidance of faculty mentors. From independent study courses to research assistantships, there are numerous opportunities for students to collaborate with faculty on scholarly projects, present their work at conferences, and publish their findings in academic journals. Speak with your academic advisor or faculty members in your area of interest to learn more about research opportunities available to you.

How can I connect with alumni and network with professionals in the field of English studies?

Our department regularly hosts networking events, alumni panels, and career workshops to connect students with alumni and professionals working in various industries related to English studies. Additionally, we encourage students to join professional organizations, attend conferences, and participate in internships to expand their professional networks and gain real-world experience. Keep an eye on our events calendar and announcements for opportunities to network with alumni and professionals in the field.
